## Epic Summary

Epic 24 delivered the final tier of performance optimizations before the deferred purpose-built storage engine (Epic 25).

1. **Zero-copy payload** (24.1) — `bytes::Bytes` for message payloads through the entire hot path, pre-allocated storage keys
2. **Scheduler sharding** (24.2) — N scheduler threads with consistent queue-to-shard hashing, ShardRouter for command routing, cross-shard ListQueues aggregation

---

## What Went Well

### Clean Two-Story Epic
Both stories shipped without CI failures or Cubic findings. The zero-copy change touched many files but was mechanical (type change + boundary conversions). The sharding change was architecturally significant but isolated behind the ShardRouter abstraction.

### Backward Compatibility Preserved
Both stories maintain backward compatibility: `bytes::Bytes` has the same wire format as `Vec<u8>`, and `shard_count=1` produces identical behavior to the pre-sharding architecture. All 500+ existing tests pass without modification.

### ShardRouter Abstraction
The routing logic is cleanly separated from the Broker. Each command variant is classified by its routing needs (queue-targeted, shard-0, broadcast, or aggregate). This makes the routing explicit and testable.
